README

CodeFury is IEEE UVCE Computer Society's Annual National Level Hackathon. Our vision is to conduct Hackathons every year to help solve some of the major problems faced by society and the industry, thus ensuring a massive impact on the world.

  • Team size - 1 to 4
  • First prize - Rs 10,000
  • Second prize - Re 5,000

Participants can sit in the comfort of their home and develop solutions to real-world problems by developing either a Web Application or an Android Application within 24 hours. Brace yourselves for one of the best events you've experienced so far. Mark your calendars on 2nd and 3rd October 2021 as we will be conducting CodeFury 4.0 on these days. Get a chance to compete against peers across the country and receive cash prizes too!

Team Alberbolt

University Visvesvaraya College Of Engineering

Winners
Web Development

Team Alberbolt

Key Features

  • Winter is coming and there is an urgent need for more sweaters, gloves, mufflers, socks, monkey caps, etc. The people who have been laid off by textile and clothing industries do have knowledge about making this kind of winter wear and also there are women with a sewing machine in their houses who can make this kind of winter wear. So we are made a website where the people who can make these winter wear have to register themselves and they have to tell us about their district and address..

  • We have made a page where the person who wants to order can sign up and login and then order the stuff that he wants. The orders are taken up by winter wear makers of the same district and we have made sure that every tailor in the district gets an equal share. When we cannot divide equally, then the person who can finish the work faster gets more orders.


Stack Used:

  • Django for backend
  • React for frontend
  • Backend hosted on Heroku
  • Backend is done mainly using the Django Rest Framework

Team Tesseract

M S Ramaiah Institute Of Technology

Runners
Web Development

Team Tesseract

Key Features:


  • Before starting with the project, we did research as to what percentage of laborers working in the unorganized sector have smartphones, and we found out that 78% of people working in these sectors, do not have smartphones.

  • So, we came up with an approach in which we can take the data of these laborers using an IVR System (Interactive Voice Response System) in which the laborer just has to give his details on the call and they are stored in the database

  • We then connect them with contractors or people who hire on our platform and they can easily get in touch with the laborers.

  • We have abided by all the 5 prompts given and the whole of our data is encrypted.

  • Major advantages of our solution

    1. No need for smartphones for the laborers
    2. Contractors can easily get in touch with their information.
    3. We have the chat feature as well and they are notified even through SMS.

About us

Computer Society is one of the most prominent societies that comes under IEEE UVCE. It acts as a great platform for students of all branches to learn more and contribute to the computing world. Computer Society has been conducting many events since its inception which includes the Annual National level hackathon CodeFury, the monthly coding competition NCode and several workshops to kindle students' interest to update their knowledge on new upcoming technological advances and improve their programming skills as well.
