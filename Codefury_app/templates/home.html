README

CodeFury is IEEE UVCE Computer Society's Annual National Level Hackathon. Our vision is to conduct Hackathons every year to help solve some of the major problems faced by society and the industry, thus ensuring a massive impact on the world.

  • Team size - 1 to 4.
  • First prize - Rs 10,000/-
  • Second prize - Rs 5,000/-

Participants can sit in the comfort of their home and develop solutions to real-world problems by developing either a Web Application or an Android Application within 24 hours. Brace yourselves for one of the best events you've experienced so far. Mark your calendars on 2nd and 3rd October 2021 as we will be conducting CodeFury 4.0 on these days. Get a chance to compete against peers across the country and receive cash prizes too!

MS Ramaiah Institute of Technology

Winners
Web Development

Team Alberbolt

Key Features

  • Pathshala is a collaborative virtual learning platform that aims to overcome the existing problems such as finding like-minded peers/groups based on specific interests, filtering through the available plethora of online resources, and not being able to be consistent enough to achieve goals.

  • To make the online group search hassle-free recommendation system is implemented wherein the student would be recommended a set of groups based on his/her weaknesses, along with a feature to form their own groups.

  • As friends help in keeping the sessions lively, Pathshala provides a feature to interact with fellow group members through chats and live streaming to promote collaborative learning.

  • The next major thing is how does one stay motivated enough to achieve the goals? And that is where Streaks’ feature comes into play. There are rewards on every 24-hour login of each student and using certain credit students can unlock the paid resources.

Team StudyChain

St Joseph's College of Engineering

Runners
Web Development

Team Tesseract

Key Features:


  • StudyChain incorporates machine learning in order to analyze the learner’s performance to map better-curated content by means of a HEAP (Heuristic evaluation and analysis of learner performance ) measure.

  • The website in order to truly support peer-to-peer learning incorporates blockchain technology to maintain the authenticity of the user by means of a rating system while maintaining user anonymity and data security.

  • Even if learners with similar aspirations are separated by physical distance, StudyChain offers video, audio, and text-based communication services to bridge the gap. Video is implemented with webRTC and Text is implemented via web sockets.

  • Time management is highly crucial for learning and hence, Pomodoro timer is embedded in our platform.

  • The platform offers paid services and provides a payment gateway. The curators can teach sessions and be paid by their peer group via Razorpay.

About us

Computer Society is one of the most prominent societies that comes under IEEE UVCE. It acts as a great platform for students of all branches to learn more and contribute to the computing world. Computer Society has been conducting many events since its inception which includes the Annual National level hackathon CodeFury, the monthly coding competition NCode and several workshops to kindle students' interest to update their knowledge on new upcoming technological advances and improve their programming skills as well.
